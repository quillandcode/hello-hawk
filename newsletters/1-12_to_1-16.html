>> System Alert: Academic Integrity & Learning Culture

I want to take a moment to acknowledge something important: the integrity in our classrooms is strong, and I'm genuinely proud of how our students approach their work. You're building something real—skills that will matter far beyond a grade on a transcript.

Here's what I want to emphasize to students and parents alike: Questions are not weakness—they are strength. When a student asks a question in class, they're not just helping themselves; they're helping at least two or three other people who had the same question but weren't sure if they should ask. Every question makes the learning environment stronger for everyone.

We're creating a space where failure is not something to hide—it's a learning opportunity. Debugging code is literally the process of learning from what didn't work. That mindset applies to everything we do. So if your student is stuck, encourage them to ask. If they're confused, remind them that confusion is the first step toward clarity.

The goal is never perfection on the first try. The goal is growth, understanding, and building the confidence to tackle harder problems over time. We're in this together.
