Weekly Computer Science Update

>> STATUS: PARTNERSHIP_REQUIRED

This week, I want to share both celebration and concern. Students are capable of excellent work, and many are showing real growth. At the same time, we are seeing a clear need for stronger follow-through with tutorials and support time—especially after our recent assessment data.

>> Course_Update: APCSA

We have completed our mid-unit test. Results show several students have gaps in understanding that can absolutely be fixed with targeted support.

The issue is not a lack of opportunity—the issue is attendance at tutorials. I offer help before school every day and after school on Tuesdays, and currently very few students are using that time.

Parent partnership is critical right now: please encourage (and when needed, require) your student to come in with specific questions about assignments, FRQs, or tests. During tutorial, I can help students finish assignments, reteach difficult concepts, and coach them on how to self-assess so their study time is focused and effective.

In this class, success requires more than attending class and completing CodeHS exercises. Students need to actively seek feedback and clarification.

>> Course_Update: Honors_CS1

CS1 is seeing the same pattern. Most students are freshmen and still learning how to manage time, advocate for themselves, and ask for help early.

As their teacher, I want to provide all possible support while they build those habits. Parent reinforcement makes a huge difference at this stage. Please help your student prioritize tutorial time, arrive with questions, and treat confusion as part of learning—not a reason to wait.

>> Course_Update: Advanced_Cloud_Computing

This group is doing a great job. We have finished the AWS AI/ML Foundations learning sequence.

Next steps:

  • Review day: brief class review, practice test, and feedback on commonly missed questions.
  • Assessment day: 50-question multiple-choice test over Modules 2–8.
  • Students will receive answer-by-answer feedback after testing.

After we complete the AI unit, we will shift to AWS Cloud Practitioner exam review to prepare for district certification vouchers, which should be available soon.

>> Academic Integrity: Support First, Accountability Always

I am taking a remediation-first approach when students make poor choices related to academic integrity. My goal is to provide enough support that students do not feel pressured to use shortcuts.

That said, repeated or habitual violations will still lead to significant consequences. Parents play a key role here. Please have direct conversations about integrity, even if you do not currently suspect a problem. Those conversations can help students make better choices and influence their peers positively.

Building a strong "no cheating" culture takes consistent messaging from both school and home.
